    Collection Companion is a project made for NYU Langone, with the purpose to help the collecting staff by answering few questions they may have regarding collection devices.
    The idea for this project came as a response to two issues:1) the constant search to improve patient care and patient experience, especially during difficult times,
    whilst minimizing discomfort and unnecessary testing; 2) the arrival of an automated specimen processor, WASP, to assist the scientist so they can perform more of the technical aspect of their duties.

    For more information regarding the WASPlab automaton, click on the following link to visit


    copan.com

    The WASP automaton will require proper collection of specimen for correct processing.
    This will allow technologists to focus on patient care, by having more time to dedicate to the isolation and identification
    of organisms found to be present in samples. It is expected that this automaton will help reduce the turn-around-time.

    This project was also done to reduce errors in collection. It's well understood that people learn in different ways.
    Some of us are visual learners. This is to hopefully assist in collections, by showing the staff, the most commonly used
    collection devices for NYU Langone Microbiology laboratory. To learn more about this project: click on the following link: An Epic Change
